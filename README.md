# Campaign Studio

### An AI Creative Director for modern brands.

Transform a product and a few inspiration images into a complete, launch-ready marketing campaign.

> **Built in ~4 hours using an AI-first workflow.**
>
> The goal wasn't to maximize features. It was to maximize product thinking, UX, and execution under a realistic startup time constraint.

# Product Thesis

Most AI marketing tools generate assets.

Creative teams don't think in assets.

They think in **campaigns**.

Campaign Studio was designed around that simple idea.

Instead of asking users to write complex prompts, the product asks them to teach the AI their visual taste using inspiration images.

The AI analyzes the creative direction, builds a campaign, explains its reasoning, and generates launch-ready variants across different platforms.

The goal isn't simply to generate images.

The goal is to become an AI Creative Director.

---

# The Problem

Launching a product today usually means:

* finding inspiration
* briefing designers
* writing captions
* creating multiple formats
* maintaining visual consistency
* repeating the process every launch

Even with AI image generators, users still have to make most creative decisions themselves.

Campaign Studio shifts AI from **content generation** to **creative direction**.

---

# My Approach

Rather than building another "Upload → Generate" interface, I focused on creating a product that feels like working with a creative partner.

Every interaction was designed around one question:

> **"How would a Creative Director think?"**

That philosophy influenced every major product decision.

---

# Core Product Decisions

## Campaigns, not generations

The product creates complete campaigns instead of isolated AI outputs.

Users work with projects, variants and campaigns—not random generations.

---

## AI analyzes before it creates

Before generating anything, the AI studies:

* composition
* lighting
* positioning
* mood
* palette
* visual hierarchy

Generation becomes the final step rather than the first.

---

## Explainability

Instead of simply returning images, Campaign Studio explains why each creative decision works.

The Inspector provides:

* creative match score
* campaign caption
* reasoning behind the design
* inspiration analysis
* suggested improvements

AI shouldn't feel like magic.

It should feel understandable.

---

## Variants over rerolls

Creative work is iterative.

Users can explore multiple directions instead of repeatedly pressing "Generate."

This creates a workflow that feels closer to real creative collaboration.

---

## Workspace-first UX

Instead of building a single demo page, I designed Campaign Studio around an actual creative workflow:

Workspace

↓

Projects

↓

Campaigns

↓

Variants

↓

Inspector

↓

Export

This makes the experience feel like software someone could use every day.
